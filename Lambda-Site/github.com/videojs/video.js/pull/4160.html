zkat commented Mar 3, 2017 •
edited

@misteroneill The purpose of acknowledging the "reverse-ism" thing is a matter of identifying where and how oppression happens in society. Heterosexual people, as people, can face oppression, but they are not oppressed in society for being heterosexual, because their heterosexuality is part of the accepted and preferred norm of the existing societal power structures. Same goes for being white, and these things go far beyond opinion: they have research and centuries of evidence backing them up.

This clause, by the way, does not preclude you from asking people to refrain from unwarranted hostility. I still don't think it's ok for people to arbitrarily attack anyone based on anything, but if a conflict happens because someone said something against a person of color, and the person of color responds with "white people are the worst", that's a situation where the person of color would not be considered to be in violation of this. It's still ok to expect people to be kind and respectful, as that benefits everyone (the WeAllJS CoC is heavily about reducing conflict temperatures -- aggressive language is still no good. It's just not considered racist/sexist/etc).

(clarification: I'm one of the authors of this CoC. Sorry to just Spanish Inquisition my way into this thread)
